Bursaries

University of the Arts London is pleased to offer a number of bursaries to those wishing to study at Postgraduate level. These bursaries are are funded by generous private donations, company donations, philanthropic charities and University Funds.

The Stationers’ Foundation Bursary

A Stanley Picker Trust bursary worth £4000 is available to one Home/EU student from an economically disadvantaged background accepted on the MA Fine Art course at Chelsea College of Art and Design.

  • The Stanley Picker Trust is a generous supporter of students in further and higher education. The bursary is valued at £4000 and will provide a contribution towards tuition fees.
  • Applications are encouraged from Home/EU students only who will benefit from postgraduate studies to realise their full potential and who are unable to finance themselves through their studies.

Applications are now closed for 2012/13.

The Stationers’ Foundation Bursary

The Stationers’ Foundation award will be a contribution towards tuition fees for one student accepted on the Masters Degree in Publishing at The London College of Communication and will cover tuition fees (year 1 only) for one student accepted on the Masters Degree in Conservation at Camberwell College of Arts.

The Financial Times Bursary

The Financial Times Bursary, worth £4000, will be offered to one Home/EU student accepted on the MA Journalism (online and television pathways) at London College of Communication.

The Wimbledon College Of Art MFA Fine Art Trust Bursary

Two bursaries have been established as a gift from the Wimbledon College Trust for two students entering the new MFA Fine Art Course at Wimbledon College of Art.

  • The two bursaries are valued at £6000 each and are for UK/EU students and will be a contribution towards tuition fees.
  • One bursary will be offered to one new student accepted or applying for the MFA Fine Art course at Wimbledon College of Art.
  • The second bursary will be offered to a student who has previously graduated from Chelsea College of Art and Design, Camberwell College of Arts or Wimbledon College of Art (CCW).

The new MFA Fine Art course at Wimbledon College of Art will support you in identifying and claiming your practice through sustained practical experimentation. The course centres on developing and refining your professional skills through creating and presenting work both at the College and off-site.

Applications are now closed for 2012/13.

The Trask Fund MRes Art Bursaries

The Trask Fund MRes Art Bursaries have been established for the three MRes Art courses at Central Saint Martins College of Arts and Design.

  • The Bursaries are valued at £2000 each and will contribute to tuition fees for first year students only.
  • The Bursaries will be offered to six students accepted on the MRes Art: Moving Image, MRes Art: Exhibition Studies and the MRes Art: Theory & Philosophy courses at Central Saint Martins College of Arts and Design.
  • Applications are encouraged from Home/EU and International students who will benefit from postgraduate studies to realise their full potential and who are unable to finance themselves through their studies.
  • The Bursaries will be awarded according to financial need and academic merit.

The MA Narrative Environments Bursary

The MA Narrative Environments bursary of £4250 will be available for 2013/14 for students on the MA Creative Practice for Narrative Environments Course at Central Saint Martins College of Arts & Design.

  • The award will cover tuition fees for year one only.
  • Applicants must ordinarily be resident in the UK.
  • The award will be assessed upon academic excellence and potential therefore no application form will be required.
  • All applicants who meet the residency criteria will be considered for the award.

In order to be considered for the award, applicants must apply for a place on the course by the beginning of May 2013. The successful applicant will be notified in writing by the end of June 2013.
