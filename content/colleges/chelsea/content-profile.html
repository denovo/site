Interview with Textile Designer Georgia Dorey

BA Textile Design, graduated 2011

Georgia Xanthe Dorey is a printed textile designer currently studying at the Royal College of Art. Interested in working towards design solutions for the future; her ideas, concepts and products are centred on both long and short term solutions. Her research is currently based on exploring human needs, and addressing the importance of encouraging user interaction through design.

Visit Georgia’s website

‘Artwork Title’, by Ann Student, LCC, MA Graphic Design Communication, 2012, photo by Joe Smith

Why did you choose BA Textile Design at Chelsea?

The Chelsea campus has a great feel to it; it is small but perfectly formed!

The studios are so bright and spacious that you actually want to spend time in them. I always felt so inspired to get to work when I walked through the door.

‘Artwork Title’, by Ann Student, LCC, MA Graphic Design Communication, 2012, photo by Joe Smith

How did you choose your course?

From a young age I had always thought that I would study fashion. After my Foundation course I started a fashion womenswear course at another university, but after a few weeks felt that it was not for me.

I took a year out to help me decide what I wanted to do. Working solidly that whole year as an intern in various fashion houses, and learning a lot about the industry, I came to the conclusion that textile design was the area I was most interested in.

I realised then, just as I do now that textiles allow you to engage fully with the body and the environment. Consequently I applied to a selection of very good universities, and ended up gaining a place at the one I wanted the most!

“I took a year out to help me decide what I wanted to do. Working solidly that whole year as an intern in various fashion houses, and learning a lot.”

Most memorable moment at Chelsea?

My most memorable moment at Chelsea was in my second year whilst on-route to Premier Vision Paris, where Chelsea had a stand to exhibit and sell our work.

I received a text message from a friend telling me that I had sold 4 samples to KENZO, all before I had even set foot off the Eurostar!

The prints then went on to be used heavily in their S/S 2011 campaign.

‘Artwork Title’, by Ann Student, LCC, MA Graphic Design Communication, 2012, photo by Joe Smith

What are you working on at the moment?

I am currently in my second year at the Royal College of Art working on my final collection for the RCA Show. I also produce work for two companies who sell my prints worldwide.

‘Artwork Title’, by Ann Student, LCC, MA Graphic Design Communication, 2012, photo by Joe Smith

Best advice you were given whilst at Chelsea?

My main tutor at Chelsea was Kathy Round. I have had a few great tutors in my creative life and she was one of the best. She taught me to follow my ideas and thoughts through to being realised.

Some of my starting points for projects can be a little obscure and she was very supportive in helping me to see that this was key to my design process.
